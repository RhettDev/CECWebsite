Frequently Asked Club Questions
What is this Curtin Engineers Club About?
The Curtin Engineers Club (CEC) is a club that provides a space for all engineering students to socialise, network and grow academically. The club runs both social and professional events throughout the year. yap yap yap
Do I need to be an engineering student to join the club?
Not at all! All Curtin students can become a member of the club no matter the discipline they are studying.
Do I need to a Curtin student to join the club?
Of course, however non-Curtin students are Associate Members of the club, rather than Ordinary Members. Don’t Worry though you still get all the same benefits!
What sort of events does the club run?
CEC runs both social and professional events throughout the school year. Social events revolve around spending time outside touching grass and connecting with other students. No engineering knowledge required. Professional events include methods of progressing your engineering career by connecting with industry reps and going on site tours   Our Events page showcases various events we have run over the years.
Do I need to be 18 or older to join?
No you do not need to be 18 to join. However the club does many 18+ events that require valid ID to be presented.

When and where are the general meetings?
CEC runs a general meeting in building 215.301 at 12pm every Wednesday. This meeting is to go over what happening in the club, receive feedback and vote on decisions.
How do I get member discounts when buying tickets via TidyHQ?
When buying event tickets on TidyHQ members receive a discount, this can be applied by logging into your account with the email you used to sign-up for the club. Additionally discount codes are emailed to members if you buy tickets anonymously.
How do I know when events are happening?
Events are advertised on our social media, links at the bottom of the page . Additionally posters of events are put up around campus and the Engineering Pavilion.
